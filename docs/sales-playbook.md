## Overview

to paid subscriber. It applies to people who submitted their email at the paywall —
they have already seen the product output and self-selected as interested.

## Part 2 — Qualified Call Script

Use this for any call booked via Touch 2 or inbound interest. Target duration: 20 min.

### Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for making time. Quick agenda: I want to show you the output from your domain
run, understand if it maps to how you actually prospect, and then you can tell me
whether it's useful. Sound good?"

### Discovery Questions (8 min)

Ask these in order. Listen. Don't pitch until you've heard answers to at least 3.

1. "How are you currently finding people to put into your outbound sequence?"
   - Listen for: manual search, VA, Sales Nav, Apollo, ZoomInfo, referrals
   
2. "When you get a lead in, what's your signal that they're worth reaching out to?"
   - Listen for: job title only, company fit, intent signals, recent activity

3. "What percentage of your outbound actually gets a reply right now?"
   - Listen for: under 5% is typical — this is your benchmark

4. "What's the most annoying part of the prospecting process today?"
   - Listen for: time, accuracy, blank slate copy, no context about why the person matters

5. "Have you ever had a rep or SDR manually search Reddit or LinkedIn for pain-signal posts?"
   - Listen for: "yes, occasionally" or "we tried it but couldn't scale it"

### Demo (5 min)

Open https://demand-signal-mvp.onrender.com. Enter their domain. Walk them through
live results. Key talking points while it runs:

- "This is searching LinkedIn and Reddit right now for people publicly expressing the
  problem you solve — not historical data, not scraped email lists."
- "The score on each card is how strongly the evidence matches your ICP."
- "Each outreach draft is anchored to the specific post we found — so you're not
  cold-emailing a stranger, you're responding to something they already said."

### Buying Intent Check (3 min)

After demo, ask these:

1. "Do any of these names look like people you'd actually want to reach out to?"
   - If yes → "That's the live product. At $199/month, you'd get 20 searches per month."
   - If no → dig into why — ICP mismatch, geography, company size?

2. "If you ran this on 10 domains this month, how many would you expect to convert
   to a call or reply?"
   - Anything above 1 → ROI math works. Surface it explicitly.

3. "What would need to be true for this to be a no-brainer for you?"
   - This surfaces the real objection. Handle it directly (see Part 3).

### Close (2 min)

"Here's what I'd suggest: start with one month at $199. Run it on 5-10 domains.
If you get one solid prospect from it, it's paid for itself. If you don't, cancel
and I'll give you a full refund — no questions. Does that work?"

## Part 3 — Objection Handling Guide

### Objection 1: "I already have Sales Nav / ZoomInfo / Apollo — why do I need this?"

**Root cause:** They think this is another lead database.

**Response:**
"Those tools give you people who match a profile. DemandSignal gives you people who
are actively complaining about the problem you solve, right now, in public. It's a
different signal — intent vs. fit. Most of our users layer it on top of Sales Nav, not
instead of it. The question is: when you pull a list of 100 VP Sales from Apollo, how
many of them are actively struggling with outbound quality today? We find the ones who
are."

---

### Objection 2: "The results don't look like my real ICP"

**Root cause:** Domain didn't return strong signals, or the ICP prompt needs tuning.

**Response:**
"That's useful feedback — let me show you how the ICP extraction works. The tool
infers your ICP from your domain homepage. If the positioning isn't crisp there,
the search can drift. Let me try a manual ICP override — what's your best one-sentence
description of who you sell to and what pain you solve?"

Then re-run with manual ICP override. If results improve, this becomes the pitch:
"That's why we built the override — you can tune the ICP on every run."

---

### Objection 3: "$199/month is too expensive for what it does"

**Root cause:** They haven't connected it to revenue impact.

**Response:**
"What's your average deal size? [Listen.] And what's your current outbound reply rate?
[Listen.] If DemandSignal gets you one extra qualified reply per month, at [their deal
size], what does that do to your CAC payback? At $199, you're paying about $2 per
search. If one search turns into one conversation, and one conversation turns into even
a small deal, the math works. What would need to happen for you to feel like it paid
for itself in month one?"

---

### Objection 4: "I don't have time to add another tool to my workflow"

**Root cause:** They're thinking about onboarding overhead.

**Response:**
"The full workflow is: type your domain, wait 90 seconds, get 5 cards with outreach
drafts. There's no integration, no CRM sync, no training. Most people run it in the
30 minutes before a prospecting session and use the output as their hit list for
that session. It replaces the time you'd spend doing a LinkedIn search — it doesn't
add to it."

---

### Objection 5: "I want to wait until you have more features / until it's more polished"

**Root cause:** Risk aversion — they're hedging against a bad purchase.

**Response:**
"Totally fair. Here's the deal: the core value — finding people who are publicly
expressing the pain you solve — is live and working today. The output you saw is the
real product. We're adding [specific near-term feature] soon, but the people who
sign up now are helping us decide what to build next, and they lock in at $199 before
we raise the price. If you wait, you'll pay more for the same signal. The risk of
waiting is higher than the risk of trying."

## Part 4 — Nurture Sequence (post-Touch 3, no conversion)

For leads who went dark after the 3-touch sequence, add to a monthly update list.
Send one email per month with:

1. A real customer story or use case (2-3 sentences)
2. One new feature or data source added
3. A soft re-invite: "If your outbound situation has changed, happy to run your domain
   again — just reply."

No more than one email per month. Goal is to stay top-of-mind for when their current
approach breaks down.
