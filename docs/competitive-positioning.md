## 1. The Five Alternatives

### 1.1 Bombora
- **Signal type:** Third-party content-consumption data aggregated across 5,000+ B2B publisher sites. "Company Surge" scores when a company's employees collectively read more than usual on a topic.
- **Buyer motion:** Enterprise sales/marketing ops layer surge scores onto existing CRM account lists; used to prioritize sequences in Salesforce/Marketo.
- **Price point:** $1,000/month starter; $20,000–$60,000+/year for full enterprise contracts.
- **Key weakness:** Signals are anonymous, company-level aggregates inferred from IP/cookie data. No individual is identified, no pain quote exists, no outreach draft is produced. Requires CRM integration before delivering any value.

### 1.2 6sense
- **Signal type:** Multi-source "dark funnel" — anonymous web visits + third-party intent + CRM signals + technographics. AI predicts which buying stage an account is in.
- **Buyer motion:** Enterprise ABM teams orchestrate paid ads, SDR alerts, and multi-channel sequences around buying-stage predictions. Requires 3–6 months of CRM/MAP onboarding plus historical data to build predictive models.
- **Price point:** Free tier (very limited); Team ~$60,000/year; Enterprise $150,000–$500,000+/year.
- **Key weakness:** Requires months of setup and is only viable for teams with dedicated RevOps. Signals are probabilistic account scores, not real humans expressing pain. Zero signal at the individual level.

### 1.3 G2 Buyer Intent
- **Signal type:** First-party G2.com activity — fires when a company visits your product page, comparison pages, or category listings on G2.
- **Buyer motion:** SDRs trigger outreach when a target account is actively comparing vendors (bottom-of-funnel). Account-level signal only; no individual identified.
- **Price point:** ~$300/month (Essentials plan); full Buyer Intent ~$1,500–$5,000+/month.
- **Key weakness:** Signal is locked to G2.com and only captures late-stage vendor comparison. Misses all early-stage pain expression. Only works if the target account is already actively shopping on G2.

### 1.4 Mention
- **Signal type:** Keyword/brand monitoring across web, Twitter/X, Reddit, news, and blogs. Users set up keyword alerts and receive a mention feed.
- **Buyer motion:** Marketing and PR teams monitor brand reputation and competitor mentions. Not a sales prospecting workflow — no prospect identification, no contact data, no ranked output.
- **Price point:** Solo $41/month; Pro $83/month; ProPlus $149/month.
- **Key weakness:** Generic alert tool requiring upfront keyword setup. Returns a raw, unranked mention feed. The gap between "someone mentioned this keyword" and "here is a sales-ready lead with outreach copy" is entirely unaddressed.

### 1.5 Apollo.io
- **Signal type:** Event-based firmographic triggers (hiring signals, funding rounds, technology installs, news mentions) overlaid on a 275M+ contact database.
- **Buyer motion:** SDRs filter the Apollo database by ICP criteria plus intent event, then launch automated cold email sequences. Core value is contact data access and sequencing, not signal detection.
- **Price point:** Free (50 email credits/month); Basic $49/user/month; Professional $79/user/month; Organization $119/user/month (annual billing).
- **Key weakness:** "Intent" signals are business events (hired a CMO, raised funding), not expressions of pain. Requires manual list-building and sequence setup. No domain-to-pain-signal flow. Outreach is not anchored to specific evidence.

## 2. The Differentiation Statement

**Unlike intent data tools (Bombora, 6sense, G2) that score anonymous account behavior, and unlike lead databases (Apollo, Clay) that require you to build a list first, DemandSignal finds the specific humans already publicly saying they have the problem you solve — from just your domain — and hands you the evidence and the outreach draft in under 90 seconds.**

The underlying logic:
- Intent scores tell you *an account might be in-market*. They never tell you *who said what*.
- Lead databases give you *people who match a profile*. They never tell you *who is hurting right now*.
- Social listening tools give you *a feed of mentions*. They never tell you *who to call and what to say*.

DemandSignal collapses signal detection, prospect identification, and outreach generation into a single zero-setup flow. The prospect self-identified their pain in public. We find them. You close them.

## 3. Top 3 Buyer Objections and Pre-emptive Responses

### Objection 1: "I already use Apollo / ZoomInfo — I have enough contacts."

**Root cause:** The buyer thinks DemandSignal is another contact database.

**Pre-emptive framing (before they raise it):**
> "DemandSignal is not a contact database. Apollo gives you people who fit your ICP profile. DemandSignal gives you people who are actively complaining about the problem you solve, right now, in public — with the post as proof. Most customers run both: Apollo for volume, DemandSignal for precision timing."

**If they raise it:**
> "When you pull 100 VP Sales from Apollo, how many of them are actively struggling with your exact problem today? DemandSignal finds the ones who are. It's a different signal — intent vs. fit. Not a replacement, a precision layer."

---

### Objection 2: "We already have a tool like Bombora / 6sense for intent."

**Root cause:** The buyer conflates content-consumption signals with pain expression.

**Pre-emptive framing:**
> "Bombora tells you a company has been reading content about a topic. DemandSignal shows you the specific person who posted: 'I'm losing $600K/year to tool sprawl and my CFO is furious.' One is a company-level score. The other is a human who has already described their problem — you're not cold-outreaching a stranger, you're responding to something they already said publicly."

**If they raise it:**
> "That signal requires months of CRM integration and produces account scores. This produces a named person, their exact pain quote, and a ready-to-send message in 90 seconds. Different product for a different motion."

---

### Objection 3: "I could just do this manually on LinkedIn or Reddit."

**Root cause:** The buyer underestimates the search effort and overestimates their capacity to find the right signal.

**Pre-emptive framing:**
> "You could. Most teams that try it either give up after 30 minutes of dead ends, or find one post and don't know if it's representative. DemandSignal searches LinkedIn and Reddit simultaneously, ranks by ICP fit and signal strength, and returns 5 cards with outreach drafts — in 90 seconds. The math: if a manual search takes 30 minutes and you need to do it for 10 domains, that's 5 hours. DemandSignal is 15 minutes of total time, including reading the cards and personalizing the drafts."

**If they raise it:**
> "How many hours per month does your team spend on this today? If DemandSignal frees up even 5 of those hours at your loaded cost, it's paid for itself before you close a single deal."

## 4. Positioning Gap DemandSignal Owns

Every competitor falls into one of two buckets:

**Bucket A — Intent scores without humans or pain context** (Bombora, 6sense, G2, Apollo)
Tells you an account might be in-market, but not *who said what* or *what problem they described*. Anonymous by design.

**Bucket B — Raw signal feeds or enrichment without signal detection** (Mention, Brand24, Clay)
Surface mentions with no path to outreach, or require you to bring your own list — zero signal detection built in.

**DemandSignal's exclusive position:**
`domain input → find specific humans publicly expressing a matched pain → ranked cards with actual evidence → pre-written outreach draft → zero setup, zero CRM, zero starting list`

No competitor in this space does all of that in a single flow. This is the moat.
